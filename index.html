By project

Scope-change trigger is the smaller of 20% of a project's BSLs or 200, CAIs included. Any project over 200 removals trips it from the file alone, and crossing it requires a New No BEAD Locations Post-Award Change with NTIA approval plus a budget modification.

For a project under 200, you may hand-enter its approved BSL count (its current approved project area, CAIs included) to test the 20% line. FAQ 9.7, p.52

The Rate ($/loc) is optional. Please hand-enter the project's approved per-location cost from your Final Proposal budget. The rate is entered per project. Leave it blank to skip the dollar estimate.

Keeping a location off the list

A keep is the exception, and the burden is on you

NTIA has run this list as the grant administrator and treats it as correct by default. To keep any location, review it and determine whether evidence exists to warrant not removing it, then carry that evidence into the consolidated Post-Award Change. Anything you do not contest removes as listed. NTIA data dictionary

For an RC3 location, a keep means evidence that the location still appears in the current Fabric, or is genuinely serviceable despite the Fabric removal. Reason Codes During the Period of Performance

Review your RC5 locations before accepting them

RC5 removals rest on service reported in the FCC map (availability as of 12/31/2025). NTIA's own guidance says presence in the BDC should not be the sole basis for removal, so these are the entries most worth checking against what you know on the ground. Where the reported service does not actually reach the location, is subsidized, or does not meet 100/20, assemble evidence that disproves the entry before it comes off. Reason Codes During the Period of Performance (RC5 may not rely solely on the FCC's BDC)

What happens next

These removals do not go in the June 30 SAR. NTIA reviews the change through a single consolidated Post-Award Change, and only after that SAR has been submitted, reviewed, and approved. NTIA data dictionary; FAQ 9.8

Any project shedding more than the smaller of 20% or 200 (CAIs included) needs a New No BEAD Locations Post-Award Change with NTIA approval, not a self-service SAR entry. FAQ 9.7, p.52; Scope Change & Budget Modification Guidance, p.4

Each affected project needs a budget modification to reflect the reduced footprint. Confirm the mechanics with your FPO before submitting, including any locations you intend to contest. FAQ 9.8; Scope Change & Budget Modification Guidance

Scope: this tool covers the recent FCC Fabric v9 NTIA per-state bespoke removal list only. It counts and flags scale only. All uncertainties should be discussed with an NTIA FPO.
